# Cover Letter Style Guide

**Author:** Benjamin Black
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** December 2024

This style guide codifies Benjamin Black's authentic writing voice for cover letter generation in the ETPS system. It is derived from analysis of approved cover letters and aligned with PRD requirements.

---

## 1. Tone Rules

### DO

- **Be direct and confident** - State qualifications assertively without hedging
- **Lead with relevance** - Open by naming the specific role and your fit
- **Show, don't tell** - Use concrete examples instead of adjectives
- **Outcome-orient everything** - Frame experience in terms of impact delivered
- **Match company register** - Mirror the formality level of the JD
- **Use active voice** - "I led" not "I was responsible for leading"
- **Keep sentences varied** - Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones
- **Close with forward momentum** - End on contribution, not gratitude

### DON'T

- ❌ Use filler phrases ("I am writing to express my interest...")
- ❌ Use fluffy adjectives ("passionate," "dynamic," "motivated," "driven")
- ❌ Hedge with weak language ("I believe I would be a great fit")
- ❌ Reference the JD explicitly ("Per your job description...")
- ❌ Use em-dashes (—) under any circumstances
- ❌ Use contractions ("I'd", "I'm", "I've") - use full forms for professional tone
- ❌ Lead with generic qualifications before role-specific ones
- ❌ Over-explain or pad word count
- ❌ Use passive constructions for achievements

## 2. Banned Phrases & Patterns

The following are **strictly prohibited** and will be rejected by the critic agent:

### Banned Opening Phrases
- "I am writing to express my interest..."
- "I am excited to apply for..."
- "I believe I would be a great fit..."
- "I am confident that..."

### Banned Adjectives
- "Passionate"
- "Dynamic"
- "Motivated"
- "Driven"
- "Fast-paced"
- "Results-oriented" (show results instead)
- "Team player" (demonstrate collaboration instead)

### Banned References
- "Per your job description..."
- "As listed in the posting..."
- "Your requirements state..."

### Banned Punctuation
- **Em-dashes (—)** - Use commas, parentheses, or restructure instead
- Excessive exclamation points
- Ellipses for trailing off

### Banned Contractions
- Use "I would" not "I'd"
- Use "I am" not "I'm"
- Use "I have" not "I've"
- Use "cannot" not "can't"
- Full forms convey a more professional tone

### Banned Closings
- "I look forward to hearing from you"
- "Please do not hesitate to contact me"
- "I am available at your convenience"

## 3. Cover Letter Structure Recipe

### Standard Template (~300 words)

```
PARAGRAPH 1: HOOK + POSITIONING (2-3 sentences, prose)
- Name the role specifically
- State your unique positioning angle
- Signal domain relevance immediately

PARAGRAPH 2: EXPERIENCE ALIGNMENT (use 2-3 bullets for skimmability)
- Open with a brief intro sentence
- Use bullet points for key qualifications with concrete outcomes
- Name specific companies/contexts for credibility
- Keep bullets concise (one line each)

PARAGRAPH 3: DIFFERENTIATOR (3-4 sentences, prose)
- Highlight a distinctive capability or project
- Connect hands-on work to strategic value
- Demonstrate curiosity and builder mindset

PARAGRAPH 4: COMPANY FIT + CLOSE (2-3 sentences)
- Reference company mission/approach specifically
- State desired contribution
- END WITH DIRECT ASK: "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my background can support [Company]'s [specific goal]."
```

### Executive Template (~250 words)

Compress to 3 paragraphs:
1. Hook + Positioning (2 sentences)
2. Experience + Differentiator (5-6 sentences)
3. Company Fit + Close (2 sentences)

### Ultra-Tight Template (~150-200 words)

Compress to 2 paragraphs:
1. Hook + Key Experience (3-4 sentences)
2. Differentiator + Close (2-3 sentences)

## 4. Sentence Rhythm Patterns

Benjamin's authentic voice uses these rhythmic patterns:

### Opening Sentences
- **Pattern A (Direct Role Reference):**
  "I'm writing to apply for the [Role] on the [Team] team."

- **Pattern B (Positioning Lead):**
  "My background spans [domain 1], [domain 2], and [domain 3] across [industries]."

### Achievement Framing
- **Pattern: Context + Action + Outcome**
  "At [Company], I [action verb] [what] by [how], [outcome/impact]."

- **Example:**
  "At Kessel Run, I supported the CIO/CDO by leading research on AI governance, data architectures, and zero-trust frameworks."

### Sentence Length Distribution
- Short (5-10 words): ~20% - for emphasis and pacing
- Medium (15-25 words): ~60% - for main content
- Long (30-40 words): ~20% - for complex ideas with context

### Connector Patterns
- "Before that..." (temporal progression)
- "I also..." (capability addition)
- "This gives me..." (value translation)
- "I bring that same..." (capability transfer)

## 5. Achievement Framing Guidelines

### Structure: WHAT + WHERE + SO WHAT

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **What** | Specific action taken | "led research on AI governance" |
| **Where** | Context/company | "at Kessel Run (DoD)" |
| **So What** | Business relevance | "supporting the CIO/CDO" |

### Strong Action Verbs (Preferred)
- Led, Built, Developed, Designed, Created
- Supported, Helped, Strengthened, Modernized
- Enabled, Accelerated, Translated, Delivered

### Weak Verbs (Avoid)
- Assisted, Participated, Contributed to
- Was responsible for, Played a role in
- Helped with (without specifics)

### Quantification Guidelines
- Include numbers when available and impressive
- Avoid vague quantifiers ("many," "various," "several")
- If no hard metrics, use scope indicators ("across all business units," "for three largest customers")

## 6. Company & Role Integration

### Weaving in Job Profile Core Priorities

For each cover letter, identify the top 2-3 JD requirements and address them explicitly:

**Pattern:**
```
"[JD Priority 1] is central to my experience at [Company], where I [specific example]."
```

**Example:**
```
JD Priority: "Strong technical grounding in data platforms and cloud ecosystems"
→ "My earlier consulting work at John Hancock, Fidelity, and other asset managers gives me a deep understanding of investment data flows, operating models, and regulatory expectations."
```

### Weaving in Company Mission

Research the company and reference their specific positioning:

**Pattern:**
```
"[Company]'s [specific approach/mission] is exactly the environment where I [value statement]."
```

**Example:**
```
"Cutter's blend of rigorous research, practical benchmarking, and direct impact on leading investment organizations is exactly the environment where I do my best work."
```

### Culture Signal Matching

| JD Culture Signal | Response Style |
|-------------------|----------------|
| "Collaborative" | Emphasize team achievements, use "we" occasionally |
| "Entrepreneurial" | Highlight builder mindset, hands-on projects |
| "Research-driven" | Reference analytical work, frameworks |
| "Client-focused" | Name specific client outcomes |
| "Innovation" | Mention emerging tech, experimentation |

## 7. Flagship Project Integration

When including a flagship project or achievement:

### Criteria for Inclusion
- Demonstrates hands-on capability (not just oversight)
- Has tangible, referenceable output (URL, system, deliverable)
- Connects to JD requirements
- Shows curiosity-driven initiative

### Framing Pattern
```
"I also [verb] [project type]. [Specific example with details]. This gives me [transferable insight]."
```

### Example
```
"I also build hands-on AI products. Most recently, I developed a production-ready RAG system (projects.benjaminblack.consulting) that enables analysts to query financial documents with grounded, explainable responses—giving me practical insight into LLM behavior, retrieval evaluation, and AI risk considerations."
```

**Note:** The example above contains an em-dash that must be replaced. Corrected version:
```
"I also build hands-on AI products. Most recently, I developed a production-ready RAG system (projects.benjaminblack.consulting) that enables analysts to query financial documents with grounded, explainable responses. This gives me practical insight into LLM behavior, retrieval evaluation, and AI risk considerations."
```

## 8. Example Outlines (Anonymized Templates)

### Template A: Consulting/Advisory Role

```
PARAGRAPH 1: HOOK
- "I'm applying for the [Role] on the [Team] team."
- "My background spans [domain area], [domain area], and [domain area] across [industry types]."
- "I've spent my career helping [client type] [value delivered]."

PARAGRAPH 2: EXPERIENCE PROGRESSION
- "At [Recent Employer], I [key achievement] by [method]."
- "Before that, through [Consulting Practice], I helped [client examples] [outcomes achieved]."
- "My earlier work at [Prestigious Companies] gives me [domain expertise]."

PARAGRAPH 3: HANDS-ON DIFFERENTIATOR
- "I also [build/create] [type of work]."
- "Recently, I [specific project] that [capability demonstrated]."
- "I bring that same [approach/mindset] to [relevant skill area]."

PARAGRAPH 4: COMPANY FIT
- "[Company]'s [specific positioning] is exactly the environment where I [value statement]."
- "I'd welcome the opportunity to [contribution] and [impact]."
```

### Template B: Technical/Product Role

```
PARAGRAPH 1: HOOK
- "I'm interested in the [Role] because [connection to background]."
- "My experience combines [technical skill] with [business skill] across [contexts]."

PARAGRAPH 2: TECHNICAL DEPTH
- "At [Company], I [technical achievement with specifics]."
- "I've worked with [technologies/platforms] to [business outcome]."
- "This includes [specific example with measurable result]."

PARAGRAPH 3: STRATEGIC TRANSLATION
- "Beyond technical execution, I [strategic contribution]."
- "At [Company], I [example of bridging technical and business]."

PARAGRAPH 4: CLOSE
- "I'm drawn to [Company] because [specific reason]."
- "I'd be excited to [contribution statement]."
```

Before generating a cover letter, ensure:

- [ ] Top 2-3 JD requirements identified
- [ ] Company mission/positioning researched
- [ ] Culture signals extracted from JD
- [ ] Most relevant experience selected
- [ ] Flagship project identified (if applicable)
- [ ] Word count target selected (Standard/Executive/Ultra-tight)

## 10. Post-Generation Validation

After generation, verify:

- [ ] No banned phrases present
- [ ] No em-dashes present
- [ ] Role named specifically in opening
- [ ] At least 2 JD requirements addressed explicitly
- [ ] Company referenced by name with specific positioning
- [ ] Closes on contribution, not gratitude
- [ ] Word count within target range
- [ ] Sentence length varied appropriately
- [ ] All claims are truthful and verifiable

## Appendix: Style Analysis Notes

### Observed Patterns from Approved Letters

**Opening Formula:**
- Names role explicitly
- States background breadth immediately
- Uses first person directly ("I'm writing," "My background spans")

**Experience Presentation:**
- Most recent/relevant first
- Groups similar experiences ("through Benjamin Black Consulting")
- Names specific companies for credibility
- Uses "before that" for temporal flow

**Closing Formula:**
- References company by name
- Cites specific company characteristic
- States "exactly the environment where I do my best work"
- Ends on contribution opportunity

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Confident but not arrogant
- Specific rather than general
- Builder mindset evident
- Curiosity-driven framing
- Executive register with accessible language
